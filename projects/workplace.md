A running record of the skills, tools, and structured learning I’ve built alongside my engineering work.
* * *

## Focus Areas

I am drawn to engineering work that improves how real systems are built, tested, and validated. A lot of that interest comes from hands-on work with fixtures, CMM inspection, process design, and finding practical ways to make production more efficient and repeatable.

Robotics stands out to me because it brings together mechanical design, electrical systems, software, and real-world problem-solving. I enjoy the challenge of working on systems where sensing, motion, controls, and reliability all have to work together.

I also enjoy AI and computer vision because I like the full cycle of the work: testing ideas, improving models, and figuring out how to make them useful outside of a notebook.

Prototyping has always been one of the most rewarding parts of engineering for me. You build, test, adjust, and learn quickly, and over time that turns into a more disciplined and repeatable way of working.

---

## Tools & Engineering Habits

- I rely on Git and clear documentation to keep projects organized, especially when the work is moving quickly or involves multiple systems and contributors.
- My approach to problem-solving is usually straightforward: isolate the issue, test carefully, validate what changed, and document the result so the learning carries forward.
- In robotics and manufacturing work, I think a lot about repeatability. Whether I am troubleshooting a robot, validating a subsystem, building a fixture, or organizing a test workflow, I want the process to be stable, understandable, and easy to hand off.
- I enjoy working close to the hardware. I like understanding how mechanical, electrical, and software issues affect each other, especially in systems that need to perform reliably in the real world.

---

## Leadership & Collaboration

- I have taken leadership roles in group engineering projects by helping with planning, task breakdown, coordination, and keeping deliverables moving.
- I value coaching and mentorship because strong teams are built through clear communication, accountability, and a willingness to help others improve.
- Some of the work I enjoy most is cross-functional. Robotics, automation, and product development often require mechanical, electrical, software, and manufacturing teams to align closely, and I like being part of that connection point.
- I work best in environments where people are building real things, solving real problems, and improving systems together.

---

## Current Goals

- Right now, I am working to strengthen my robotics and AI development workflow so the work is not just functional, but also reproducible, well-structured, and easier to deploy.
- I want to keep improving how I test and validate robotics and embedded systems, especially where hardware, software, and reliability all meet.
- I am also continuing to build my experience in quality operations and scalable engineering workflows, particularly in environments where speed, discipline, and technical depth all matter.
- Over time, I want to keep growing toward work that sits at the intersection of robotics, intelligent systems, manufacturing, and practical engineering execution.
